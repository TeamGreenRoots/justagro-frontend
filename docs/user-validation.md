# User Validation Report – JustAgro

## Objective

The goal of this research was to understand how farmers currently receive payments, the challenges they face, and whether they would adopt a digital payment solution.

---

## Method

We conducted user surveys with farmers and gathered supporting insights from existing research on agricultural payments and mobile money in Africa.

---

## Key Findings from User Research

### 1. Payment delays are common

Most farmers reported frequent delays when receiving payments, often leading to disputes.

### 2. Delays affect operations

Late payments make it difficult for farmers to plan, restock, or manage finances.

### 3. Strong interest in a solution

Many farmers are willing to try a digital tool that ensures faster and more reliable payments.

### 4. Most requested features

* Instant payment alerts
* Delivery confirmation tracking
* Transaction history
* Credit history

---

## Insights from Secondary Research

1. Farmers in Africa face delayed payments and limited access to financial services, affecting cash flow.
2. Mobile payments enable faster and more secure transactions, reducing reliance on cash.
3. Many farmers still rely on cash despite having mobile phones, showing a gap in adoption.
4. Payment delays impact trust and expectations between farmers and buyers.

---

## Key Insights

1. Payment delays are frequent and significant.
2. Farmers want faster and more transparent payment systems.
3. There is a gap between current tools and user needs.
4. Trust and visibility are critical.

---

## Conclusion

The findings show strong alignment between user feedback and existing research. Farmers face real challenges with delayed payments and are open to digital solutions that improve speed, transparency, and record-keeping.

---

## Product Implications

* Focus on simple and reliable payment processes
* Provide clear notifications and tracking
* Build trust through transparency
